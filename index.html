Motorcycles evolved from late 19th-century experiments adding steam, then gasoline engines to bicycles, culminating in the 1885 Daimler-Maybach Reitwagen, the first internal combustion motorcycle. Early 20th-century models grew in popularity for transport, leading to the rise of major brands like Harley-Davidson, Triumph, and BMW, with the industry shifting towards Japanese dominance from the 1950s.
